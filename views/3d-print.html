Introduction

During our time in class, we had the opportunity to learn about the 3d-printing side of computer science and engineering. We learned how to make our own 3d printed objects that would help us in the everyday world. Overall, this unit taught me how to utilize a 3d printer to create objects that could help us and the process behind making these objects.

My Design

For my project, I decided to design something that can help me a little bit with my Diabetes. I couldn't do something so in depth to actually help my Diabetes, but I could make something to seucre my diabetic equipment better. What I designed was a case for my Diabetic sensor. For the first part, I knew I had to create a hinge system which would allow me to open and close a lid, so I worked away and utilized the sources on Tinkercad and the measurements of my sensor to develop the lid and hinges.

Lastly, I had to create an actual container utilizing the height and width of the sensor so that it could fit inside. So I used a rectangular shape to hold it, adjusting it to the perfect size so that the sensor could fit in, and for the ability for me to place some cushoning inside of it.

In the end, I was able to print a successful case and hinge system to connect onto the main case. I had to make a small adjustment to the rod I printed in order to make it actually stay put and not fall out. I also had to add the cushoning onto my project, nut in the end, it was a success!
